    Class Presentation: Interrupted Time Series Analysis

    What to Say When the Professor Asks

    Your Data

    “My dataset consists of daily stock prices for four major sports betting companies: DraftKings (DKNG), Penn Entertainment (PENN), Caesars Entertainment (CZR), and MGM Resorts (MGM). The data spans from January 2020 to December 2024, giving me approximately 5 years of daily observations.”

    The Intervention

    “My intervention point is January 8, 2022 - the date when New York State launched online sports betting.”

    Why This Intervention Matters

    “New York quickly became the largest sports betting market in the United States by revenue, representing about 20% of the total U.S. online sports betting market. This was a major industry milestone that all four companies in my dataset participated in, making it a clear, well-defined intervention that would plausibly impact stock valuations.”

    The Research Question

    “I’m examining whether the New York legalization produced a significant change in either the level (immediate jump/drop in stock prices) or the trend (long-term growth trajectory) of these sports betting stocks.”

    Technical Details (If Asked)

    Time Periods

    • Pre-intervention: January 2020 - January 7, 2022 (~2 years, ~500 trading days)
    • Post-intervention: January 8, 2022 - December 2024 (~3 years, ~750 trading days)

    Model Specification

    “I’m using a standard interrupted time series regression model that includes:”

    1. A time trend variable (days since start)
    2. A binary intervention indicator (0 before Jan 8, 1 after)
    3. A time-since-intervention variable to capture trend changes

    The model equation is:

    Price = β₀ + β₁(Time) + β₂(Intervention) + β₃(Time_Since_Intervention) + ε

    What Each Coefficient Tells Us

    • β₁ = Pre-intervention trend (how stocks were growing before NY)
    • β₂ = Immediate level change (did stocks jump/drop on Jan 8, 2022?)
    • β₃ = Trend change (did growth accelerate or slow after NY legalization?)

    Why This Is NOT COVID-Related

    “While my data does include the COVID period, the intervention I’m studying - New York’s sports betting legalization - occurred in January 2022, well after the initial COVID shock. This is a regulatory policy change, not a pandemic-related event.”

    Expected Findings (Hypotheses)

    Hypothesis 1: Positive Level Change

    “I expect to see a positive immediate level change (β₂ > 0) because the market would have priced in the positive news of the largest U.S. market opening.”

    Hypothesis 2: Potential Trend Change

    “The trend change (β₃) could go either way:” - Positive: If NY success led to expectations of more states legalizing - Negative: If initial euphoria wore off as companies faced NY’s high tax rates (51%)

    Visual Summary

    “In my analysis document (ITS_analysis.html), I have:”

    1. Time series plots for all 4 stocks with the intervention date marked in red
    2. Regression tables showing the statistical significance of level and trend changes
    3. Fitted vs. actual plots to visualize how well the model captures the intervention effect
    4. Comparative summary showing which companies were most affected

    Key Strengths of This Intervention

    ✅ Clear, specific date: January 8, 2022 (no ambiguity) ✅ Exogenous shock: Government policy, not company-specific ✅ Sufficient pre/post data: 2 years before, 3 years after ✅ Major economic significance: Largest U.S. sports betting market ✅ Not COVID-related: Regulatory change in 2022 ✅ Affects all companies: All 4 stocks have NY operations

    If Asked About Confounders

    “The main potential confounders would be:”

    1. Other state legalizations happening around the same time → But NY was by far the largest
    2. Market-wide factors (recession, interest rates) → The ITS model controls for general trends
    3. Company-specific events (earnings, M&A) → We’re looking at industry-wide patterns across 4 companies

    Bottom Line

    “The New York sports betting legalization provides a natural experiment to test whether a major market expansion causally affected sports betting stock prices. The interrupted time series framework allows me to separate the immediate market reaction from longer-term trajectory changes.”

    File Locations

    • Analysis document: ITS_analysis.qmd (source code)
    • Rendered HTML: docs/ITS_analysis.html (open in browser)
    • Data files: data/financial/DKNG_daily.csv, etc.

    Practice Talking Points

    1. “My data is daily stock prices for four sports betting companies from 2020-2024.”
    2. “The intervention is January 8, 2022 - when New York launched online sports betting.”
    3. “New York became the largest U.S. sports betting market, so this was a major industry event.”
    4. “I’m testing whether this caused an immediate jump in stock prices and/or changed the growth trajectory.”
    5. “This is not COVID-related - it’s a 2022 regulatory policy change.”

    Confidence Boosters

    ✅ Your intervention is valid - it’s a clear, specific date with economic significance ✅ Your data supports the analysis - you have sufficient pre/post observations ✅ Your methodology is standard - interrupted time series is the right approach ✅ Your topic is interesting - sports betting is timely and relevant ✅ You’re well-prepared - you have visualizations, tables, and statistical results
